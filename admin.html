📖 How to use this app (for admins) ▾
The basic flow
  1. Set the rules (this Settings tab): number of rounds, pairing mode (Scramble = random each round, or Swiss = pair by standings), optional top cut, tie-breaker, and the faan scoring table. Press Save.
  2. Add players (Players tab): Add Single one at a time, or Import List to paste many names. Then Check In the players who are present — only checked-in players who haven't been marked Did Not Finish get paired.
  3. Pair a round (Pairings tab): press Generate Round. Round 1 is random; later rounds follow your pairing mode.
  4. Enter scores (Scorekeeping tab): pick the round and table, type each player's net points (they must add up to zero per table), and Submit. To enter a whole round at once, use Bulk Score Import — one row of 4 scores per table.
  5. Pair the next round → enter its scores → repeat. Standings update automatically.
Substitutes

Substitutes are a filler pool used to round the real players up to a multiple of 4. When you're short, the app offers to add subs (named SUBSTITUTE n); extra subs are benched automatically. Note: a sub who actually plays a hand does count in scores and standings.

Swapping & substituting mid-round

On the Pairings tab, Swap / Substitute edits seats in an already-generated round. Pick two players:

  • Both seated → their seats are exchanged.
  • One seated + one on the bench → the bench player takes the seat; the other becomes benched. This is how you bring a substitute in for someone who left.

Swapping does not auto-mark the outgoing player — if they withdrew, set them Did Not Finish on the Players tab so they're left out of future pairings. If the table was already scored, the score stays with the original player ID — re-enter it for the incoming player.

Fixing a round

Regenerate Latest Round (Pairings tab) deletes the latest round's pairings and its scores, then re-pairs the current active players — useful after a player is marked Did Not Finish, changing who should be playing.

Colour guide for buttons: green = normal actions · orange = bigger actions (generate, regenerate, swap, bulk import, new tournament) · red = destructive (clear / delete).

Tournament Rules & Settings

Faan Scoring

Holds the tournament in a check-in phase before it officially starts: you pair Round 1 in advance and only lock it in once you know who showed up. When you Begin, anyone not checked in is dropped and Round 1 is condensed to fill the empty seats. Leave off to start immediately with the full roster.
After a chosen round, the top players split off into a separate finals bracket and play only each other for the rest of the tournament; their final placing is decided by the points they earn from the cut onward. Set how many players advance and which round the cut happens after.
If set to 0, cut triggers manually or immediately.
